Rookie Class Predictions - 2019

Rookie Projections - Statement of Purpose and Methods

What were we trying to do?

We analyzed statistics from the last 20 rookie draft classes to predict the stats of the 2019 NBA rookie draft class. In order to do this we scraped draft orders, college, and NBA statistics for 718 former college players (excluding international and high school players) who played in the NBA. Next we used the college basketball statistics we obtained for these historic players to create a prediction, which we then compared against their rookie seasons. We utilized 29 unique statistical categories related to each players statistical production and implemented recursive feature elimination to determine which feature had the highest impact. Three different regression models were run and analyzed to determine which model was best for our prediction. Ulitmately, we chose Random Forrest.

Random Forrest held the highest % accuracy within 50% of the predicted values. Additionally, it performed higher than the other models in cross validation testing. Random Forrest outperformed Linear Regression and XGB Regressor in predicting high level performers, namely players that scored more than 15 points per game in their rookie seasons.

Overall, their are still improvements that can still be made to our model. We experienced overfitting initially due to the size of our dataset and had concerns with the low accuracy of our predictions. Eventually, we determined that overfitting our training models allowed us to more accurately predict the stats of higher perfoming players.
